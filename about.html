I’m a backend-focused software engineer who enjoys making complex systems feel simple, steady, and useful.

At Victaulic, I help modernize legacy internal platforms into more scalable services using NestJS, Angular, SQL, and Azure. My work includes refactoring tightly coupled systems, improving background processing reliability, and supporting production integrations across teams and external platforms.

In parallel, I’m building AISeq, where I lead the architecture of a wellness genomics platform using TypeScript, PostgreSQL, Prisma, and clean architecture principles. That means designing versioned data models, defining clear system boundaries, and building APIs for structured, workflow-driven user experiences.

I’m most interested in backend-heavy engineering work involving APIs, workflow systems, integrations, and data-oriented platform design. I like solving problems at the intersection of product, operations, and engineering, and I care a lot about building systems that are reliable, maintainable, and easy for people to work with.

Outside of work, I like traveling, photography, and cars, especially older BMWs and Porsches. I spend time working on my own old E46 BMW too, which fits pretty naturally with the part of my brain that likes digging into systems and figuring out how they work.
